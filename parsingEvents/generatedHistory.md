# Timeline of the World

## Egypt

**Pyramids**
*2550-2490 BC*
The Pyramids and other tombs are massive marvels of engineering, built surprisingly early in Egyptian history by warring rulers who liked to flex in an attempt at legitimacy.

**Hatshepsut**
*1473-1458 BC*
A rare female Pharaoh, Hatshepsut focuses on trade and building projects for a peaceful and prosperous reign. She was erased and slandered after her death.

**Akhenaten and the Aten**
*1353-1336 BC*
Akhenaten forces massive cultural, political and religious changes, privileging the sun-disk (Aten) to the destruction of other gods. His cult is widely hated and dies alongside him.

**Cleopatra**
*50-30 BC*
Highly intelligent, educated and magnetic, Cleopatra allied with Caesar to defeat her brother-husband and become sole Pharoah. She ruled actively and benevolently before being killed in a Roman succession crisis.

## Assyria

**Tablets of Kanesh**
*1900-1700 BC*
Our understanding of Ancient Anatolia comes from a collection of clay tablets found at Kanesh, the letters and records of a town of traders who built on family ties to create complex economic systems of debt and ownership.

**Esarhaddon**
*681-669 BC*
Paranoid, Esarhaddon would perform 'Substitute King' 7 times in his reign to go farm. Still, he managed to conquer Egypt and rebuild Babylon before leaving a strong realm behind in a smooth transition.

## Carthage

**Queen Dido**
*814 BC*
Fleeing her murderous brother, the King of Tyre, Dido was crowned in Carthage after cunningly creating the country out of cowhide. She cameos in the Aeneid as a temporary love interest.

## England

**Robin Hood**
*1199-1216 AD*
Richard Lionheart abandons duty to Crown for duty to Crusade, so bro John sneakily takes the reigns. Robin Hood rebels in accordance with popular sentiment, and John's eventual Kingship becomes pyrrhic when it's followed with the Magna Carta.

**War of the Roses**
*1455-1487 AD*
Go watch House of the Dragon for a semi-accurate recreation.

**South Sea Bubble**
*1720 AD*
Hype for riches builds as British politicians are bribed into the South Sea Company. Govt debt gets handed over alongside the everyman's life savings, until it all pops.

**Steam Powered Loom**
*1786 AD*
The Industrial Revolution kicks off properly with the invention of an automatic loom, accompanied by very controversial job loss. The Luddites protest loudly as the Marxist era of capitalism properly kicks off.

## New Zealand

**Early sea voyages**
*2500-1300 BC*
Early migration of Lapita culture from Taiwan island-hopped through the Philippines, Papua New Guinea, Melanesia. Voyages went 30-300 km and took 1-4 days, with continuous evidence of land proximity.

**Early ocean voyages**
*1300-800 BC*
Migrating eastwards meant canoeing 500-2000 km, from 4 days to 3 weeks. Polynesians created sophisticated navigation: star lines, swell refraction patterns, migration patterns, string web mapping. Occupation of Vanuatu, Fiji, Tonga, Samoa, etc.

**New Zealand's 'discovered'**
*1769-70 AD*
English Captain Cook and French Captain de Surville sail to NZ at the same time. de Surville dies soon, Cook survives; circumnavigates and charts “New Zealand” which labels him 'founder'.

**Great ocean voyages**
*800-1200 AD*
400 years of extraordinary Polynesian ocean exploration, crossing 1500-8000km per voyage, taking 2 months between landfall. Occupied entire Pacific; probably reached South America given Kumara distribution.

**Kupe**
*1200 AD*
Sailing from Hawaiki in his waka Matahorua, Kupe discovered Aotearoa. First landed at Hokianga before circumnavigating Te Ika-a-Māui (North) and northwest of Te Waipounamu (South). Returned to Hawaiki.

**The Great Fleet**
*1250-1300 AD*
Many of Kupe's grandkids's gen migrated to New Zealand, with up to 40 legendary vessels with family/tribal groups fleeing resource competition and conflict at home. Each established control over various territories.

**Abel Tasman**
*1642 AD*
Tasman “discovers” “Nova Zeelandia”, 4 Dec 1642, sailing from Jakarta seeking new trade opportunities for the Dutch East India Company. Anchored near Golden Bay but didn't land as four sailors were killed in fight with Māori.

**Increasing European contact**
*1787-1840 AD*
In 1787, Australia becomes a British penal colony. NZ <> Europe trade increases: timber, flax, food. More over time: missionaries, whale/seal oil/furs. further grew shipping and coastal trade. 

**Musket Wars**
*1810-1835 AD*
European trade introduces guns to the already-warlike Maori, escalating existing conflicts between tribes into an arms race. Longer campaigns (sustained by potatoes) killed 20% of Maori.

**Independence**
*1834 AD*
1835: 52 (mostly northern) tribes signed He Whakaputanga o te Rangatiratanga o Nu Tireni ( Declaration of Independence of the United Tribes of NZ). 1836: British Parliament acknowledges.

**Darwin visits**
*1835 AD*
Charles Darwin visits on board the Beagle in 1835. By then, 3-5 sailing vessels anchored at Kororāreka (Russell, Bay of Islands) were a common sight

**The New Zealand Company**
*1839 AD*
The New Zealand Company bought land for trinkets to on-sell for huge margins. EG Wakefield snaked ahead of Royal law to colonize, with a plan he'd made while in prison for kidnapping and marrying a 15-year-old heiress. 

**Treaty of Waitangi**
*1840 AD*
6 Feb 1840: Te Tiriti o Waitangi established the relationship between the Crown and Māori. 540 Maori signed the Te Reo treaty. 24 Chiefs signed an English version. There were… differences.

**Wairau, Wellington, Wanganui**
*1843-1847 AD*
June 1843. Dodgy land deals by NZCo cause Wairau Incident: Te Rauparaha vs Arthur Wakefield. AW + 21 settlers killed, 4 Māori dead. Further fights in Wellington, Wanganui. Gov Fitzroy's inquiry found for the Māori, raising colonists' anger.

**Flagstaff War**
*1845-1846 AD*
Hōne Heke was first to sign Te Tiriti o Waitangi & donated national Flagstaff. But later Crown usurpation of Māori authority got so bad he chopped the Flagstaff down 4x. Led to battles and fortification in Auckland.

**Taranaki and Waikato**
*1860-1866 AD*
Settler pop overtakes Māori. Governance now local instead of Crown Office. Māori unite in Kingi movement in protest, block land sales. Dodgy deal pretext for 19k trained settler troops to battle 3k Māori, then alienate Māori from confiscated NI land.

**New Zealand (a.k.a. Land) Wars**
*1843-1898 AD*
New Zealand (Land) Wars: Brits ignore their own treaty due to racism and greed. Two decades later Māori fight to assert their treaty rights against the Crown, the New Zealand Company, etc. Turns into British war of conquest.

**Parihaka**
*1881 AD*
Te Whiti and Tohu leads village Parihaka in nonviolent resistance. Attracts Māori from all NZ, global renown, influences Gandhi. Invaded on 5 Nov 1881. The government troops applied scorched earth tactics for three years to stifle the movement.

## Netherlands

**Tulip Bubble**
*1634-1637 AD*
Quick to bloom, quicker to doom. Exotic tulips signalled status, causing one of the earliest speculative bubbles as all Amsterdam spent big on bulb bonds.

## Byzantium

**Emperor Justinian**
*527-565 AD*
Weds 'actress' Theodora. Rebel Nika riots, plague, war. Persia peace and reconquered Rome(+Egypt) imperiled by shaky trust in Belisarius. Lasting legislative legacy: efficient taxes and Corpus Juris Civilis. Hagia Sophia, Monophysite, feminism.

## Rome

**Punic War 2**
*218 BC*
Hannibal Jr leads Carthage to victory against Rome in reprise war with surprise elephants. Despite with Senate resistance, Scipio innovates back. “Cowardly” tactics gets Rome the final victory. Much genius new military tactics (eg: envelopment) on both sides.

**Social War**
*90-89 BC*
City-state Rome has hegemony over Italy. Non-citizens are legally lesser and upset about it. Italian cities seeking integration or independence form a coalition. The Social War results in a unified polity across Italy with wider citizenship.

**Julius Caesar**
*50 BC*
Civil war, land reform, massive conquests

**Pompeii**
*79 AD*
Mt Vesuvius erupts in 79AD, burying Pompeii in mud and ash. Sad as it was for the populace at the time, later historians are grateful for this snapshot of Roman life.

**Crisis of the 3rd Century**
*235-284 AD*
Rome's ongoing decline tips to outright catastrophe. Fatal inflation as denarii lose silver. Coups, killings, 30+ emperors in 50 years: fragmented power. Diocletian's East/West Tetrarchy solution admits empire's centralization has disintegrated.

## Achaemenid

**Cyrus the Great**
*560-530 BC*
Conquers the Golden Crescent into a Golden Age, with religious tolerance and local self-governance. He declares human rights on the Cyrus Cylinder and becomes Messiah by freeing the Jews.

**Darius I**
*522 BC*
Cyrus's son Bardiya was slain by his brother in secret, allowing a Magian impersonator to claim the throne. Thus t'was righteous vengeance when guardsman Darius I slew the imposter. Alternatively, a skeptic might call him a king-slaying ursurper.

## Ethiopia

**Queen of Sheba**
*9500 BC*
Folklore holds that King Solomon seduced the African Queen of Sheba, who returned to Ethiopia for their son Menelik to found the Solomonic dynasty, making the country a rare Christian holdout in Africa.

**Gudit**
*980 AD*
A Warrior Queen claims Ethiopia and rules for 40yrs, her descendants ruling after. She's a muddled figure in myth, a church-burning possibly-Jewish possibly-pagan conqueror. Her dynasty is considered a Dark Age external to Ethiopia's historical flow. 

**Kebra Nagast**
*1350 AD*
A religious bloodline epic serving to establish the Ethiopian monarchs as direct descendants of Solomon. It is skillfully woven with the historical record, but likely largely invented in search of divine legitimacy after a Crown-by-Conquest.

**Mentewab**
*1730 AD*
Co-regnant with her son, and holds power peacefully for decades, until her Oromo DIL contests the regency for her grandson. Their powerful families fight each other to such weakness that outsider Mikael Sehul can snake control, causing an interregnum.
